# Fort White Casons — Record Search (Thadeous → Carl → Robert Sr.)

A deep record search on the three Fort White, Columbia County, Florida ancestors on
the direct line, focused on the **land-descent question**: is there a will, deed, or
federal homestead patent documenting how the Fort White / CR 778 land was acquired by
Thadeous (~1888) and passed **Thadeous → Carl → Robert Sr.**?

Run via the multi-source research harness (fan-out web search → fetch → 3-vote
adversarial verification). **72 claims upheld (63 high-confidence), 3 refuted**, across
~19 primary/secondary sources. Every fact below is tied to a checkable source; nothing
is asserted without one. Compiled genealogies are marked **secondary** — they are
leads to confirm against primary vitals, not proof.

## Two open lines opened (surface in the "Open Lines" worklist)

1. **Marriage date — Thadeous & Georgia.** The Tustenuggee headstone reads *"Married
   Georgia Q. on 12/25/1883,"* but our record says **Christmas Eve 1882**. Off by a
   year (and a day). → Columbia Co. marriage return settles it. (Georgia also shows
   **1866**–1937 on Find a Grave vs **1860** in our record.)
2. **Thadeous → Carl parentage + the land.** Our record (and the DuBose genealogy)
   name Thadeous as Carl's father, but **no primary record yet places Carl in
   Thadeous's household** — and Thadeous's Find a Grave memorial lists only *four*
   children, Carl not among them. → The **1900/1910/1920 census** of the Fort White
   household, and Thadeous's **1945 probate**, should both name Carl and say who took
   the land. This is the load-bearing link for the land descent.

---

## The land-descent answer

**No will, deed, or homestead patent has been located online yet** — but the records
**survive** and the repositories are pinned. The single highest-value pull is
**Thadeous's 1945 Columbia County probate file**: if it exists it likely names the
Fort White land *and* which son took it, documenting the land **and** the
Thadeous→Carl link in one record.

Note: only ~40% of homestead applicants ever patented, so a **null BLM-GLO result does
not mean "no land"** — it likely means the land came by **cash sale or private deed**
(Columbia Co. deeds survive from **1875**).
