// narrative · audit-corrected

Documented as a headright of Capt. Thomas Harwood on the patent of 7 July 1635 (Patent Book 1, p.124) — the strongest primary anchor for his arrival in Virginia. A 1642 deposition records his age as 38, yielding a birth around 1604. By the early 1640s he is an established planter in Lynnhaven Parish, married to Elizabeth (Keeling) Leighton, widow of William Leighton, acquiring the 100-acre Keeling patent in her right of dower. He died 1651; his widow received letters of administration 15 April 1652; the inventory of 26 June 1652 listed 28,170 lbs of tobacco — appraised by Burroughs, Hall, Davis, and Edward Cannon (the root of the long-running Cason/Cannon homoglyph conflation in derivative trees).

▸ Priority 01 · LOAD-BEARING

The Gen 5 hole

72-year span between William⁴ Cason (~1691) and Ransom Cason Sr. (~1763) across one nominal link. Implausible as one generation. Leading candidate: William⁴'s son James (c.1727) — landless, no surviving deeds. Cannon Sr. and William Jr. eliminated by wills and migration paths. Hillery placed in interior GA, not coastal — unlikely.
Pitt County, NC estate files, tax lists, and deed books 1750–1800. repo: NCDAH · microfilm C.075.70001 · original images, not abstracts
Y-DNA triangulation across William⁴'s six documented sons' lines. repo: FamilyTreeDNA · Cason Surname Project
Source citation for "James Jr. (c.1750) is Ransom's brother" claim. repo: WikiTree Cason-543 · chase the citation to its primary
▸ Priority 02 · ORIGIN

Thomas Casson's English origin

Digswell is disproven. The surname's heartland is Lincolnshire / Cambridgeshire / Norfolk. "Casson" specifically clusters in northern England — Lancashire / Yorkshire / Westmorland — and may be a distinct surname. The Virginia land acquisition came through marriage, not transportation, so port records may be silent.
Parish registers · Casson/Cason/Cawson baptisms 1595–1610 across Lincs/Cambs/Norfolk. repo: Findmypast · FreeREG · county record offices
Casson registers across the northern dioceses. repo: Lancashire Online Parish Clerks · Borthwick Institute
Capt. Thomas Harwood's other 1635 headrights — surname-cluster geographic origins. repo: VA Patent Book 1 · Library of Virginia
TNA PCC will D883300 · "John Cason, Grocer of London". repo: The National Archives, Kew
▸ Priority 03 · ETHICAL

Names of enslaved people held by the family

Thomas Sr.'s 1652 estate (28,170 lbs tobacco) was built on enslaved labor; the surviving inventory fragments do not name individuals. The same gap repeats in Silas Cason's 1850/1860 Florida slave schedules. Reconstructing identity is both an ethical obligation and frequently the only surviving evidence of household composition.
Madison Co. FL probate packets 1862–65 (Silas Cason estate inventory). repo: Madison Co. Courthouse · inventories name enslaved individuals
1870 Federal Census, Madison Co. — Black households adjacent to Cason / Carlton families. repo: NARA M593
Lower Norfolk Order Books 1640–70 — searches for property transactions naming enslaved persons. repo: Library of Virginia microfilm
// 06 · SIBLING BRANCH

The Hillery → Utah line

A parallel spine. William⁴'s son Hillery (Revolutionary War Patriot, DAR #A216752) migrated to interior Georgia in 1792 — not coastal Glynn where Ransom Sr. settled. His descendants ran a different arc: GA → IL → AR with John Dennis (1829), then FL with Silas, then westward to Iron County, Utah with George Tompkies — severing 250 years of Southern roots into LDS pioneer stock.
